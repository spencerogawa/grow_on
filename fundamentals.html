Am I Suited for the farm business?

Ask yourself the following questions and see if you think you have the right personality and attitude towards becoming an organic farmer.

01

Do you enjoy eating fresh foods all the time?

You are what you eat

02

Are you passionate about sustainability of natural resources?

Our resources are not limitless. Many industry professionals are committed to work on our sustainable future.

03

Do the effects of global warming on the environment concern you, and wondering how to help prevent it through your job/career?

Canada’s industry is a leader internationally, ranking 1st among G7 countries for the lowest food procession costs.

04

Are you well organized and capable of planning ahead with full autonomy?

It’s great being your own boss, being able to take time for family events, and having a flexible schedule.

05

Do you like to see tangible results and progress of your work?

You can literally see the fruits of your labour right in front of you.

06

Are you willing to seek out the necessary advice in order to gain the personal skills and business experience of becoming a farmer?

None of us are born professionals at anything, so we need a helping hand here and there.

4 tips to start a farm business

01.
TRY IT OUT

Organic farming is a very skillful and technical professional field. So it is a good idea to start it small to minimize the mistakes you make, and maximize the successes. Start by making a garden on your balcony, porch or backyard. There are also many shared community gardens if you live in the city but want a bigger space. Get to know what types of crops are suited for you and excite you. Not ready to take the full leap yet? Many farms and farmers offer internship and volunteer programs to dip your toe in the water. To find out more, download the Grow ON app.

02.
GAIN AGRICULTUAL KNOWLEDGE

There are many farmers who have tons of knowledge and experience on farming and are willing to pass on their wisdom. Workshops and educational programs are a great source of information. Also remember that technology isn’t only advancing in urban areas, but also on the farmlands. The agricultural industry is advancing day by day as we discover more efficient and sustainable ways of farming, so it is important to learn about the cutting edge technology that will shape the future.

03.
MANAGE YOUR FINANCES

Don’t think that you have to come up with capital to start a farm by yourself. There are many grants and available funds dedicated for those starting up a farm provided by many private and government organizations. Of course it is important to understand the investments necessary but also to recognize market trends that will shape your potential revenue. Having a sound knowledge of where your money comes and goes is the key to a successful business. For more information on grants and available funds download the Grow ON app.

04.
LEARN HOW TO TELL YOUR STORY

As a business owner, marketing yourself is extremely important especially as society trends towards social media and digital marketing . Pick a medium that works for you whether it be via video, photo or blogging. Get comfortable sharing your days and values that will make you stand out in the market. Be honest, kind and open minded, share your stories, victories and even failures. This is something you can start right away to help and grow your future farming career.
